# 🧠 Major Branches of Psychology

Psychology is a vast subject divided into specialized areas depending on what is being studied. These branches show **where psychology is applied in real life**.

---

## Overview

| Branch | What It Studies |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Clinical** | Mental disorders and therapy |
| **Educational** | Learning and teaching |
| **Social** | Behavior in groups |
| **Developmental** | Human growth across lifespan |
| **Industrial / Organizational** | Psychology in workplaces |
| **Cognitive** | Thinking and mental processes |
| **Biological** | Brain and nervous system |

---

## 1️⃣ Clinical Psychology

Deals with **mental health problems and psychological disorders**.

Helps people with conditions like anxiety, depression, trauma, and personality disorders through **therapy and counseling**.

> **Role:** Diagnose and treat mental illnesses such as Depression and Anxiety Disorder.

---

## 2️⃣ Educational Psychology

Studies **how people learn** and how to improve that process.

Focus areas:
- Learning methods & memory
- Student motivation
- Teaching strategies

> **Example:** Studying why some students learn faster than others — and using that to improve teaching.

---

## 3️⃣ Social Psychology

Studies **how people behave in groups** and how others influence our attitudes, decisions, and behaviors.

Topics include: peer pressure, group behavior, social influence.

> **Key insight:** People often behave differently in groups than when they are alone.

---

## 4️⃣ Developmental Psychology

Studies **how humans grow and change throughout life** — from infancy to old age.

Covers: infancy → childhood → adolescence → adulthood → old age

> **Notable figure:** Jean Piaget studied how children's thinking develops over time.

---

## 5️⃣ Industrial / Organizational Psychology (I/O)

Applies psychology to **workplaces and companies**.

Studies: employee motivation, productivity, leadership, and job satisfaction.

> **Goal:** Help companies improve team performance and work environments.

---

## 6️⃣ Cognitive Psychology

Studies **how the mind processes information**.

Focus areas: thinking, memory, perception, decision making, problem solving.

Connects strongly with **Artificial Intelligence** and **Neuroscience** — many modern technologies attempt to simulate human thinking.

---

## 7️⃣ Biological Psychology

Studies **how the brain and nervous system influence behavior** — connecting psychology with Biology.

Examines: brain structure, hormones, neurons, nervous system.

> **Goal:** Understand how brain activity affects emotions, thinking, and behavior.

---

## 💻 Most Relevant Branches for CS Students

| Branch | Why It Matters in CS |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Cognitive** | How humans think → AI, machine learning |
| **Social** | How people interact with systems and apps → UX design |
| **Industrial / Organizational** | Teamwork dynamics in tech companies |
