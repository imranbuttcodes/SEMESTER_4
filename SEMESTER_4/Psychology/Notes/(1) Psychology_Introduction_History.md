## 📖 Definition of Psychology

**Psychology is the scientific study of behavior and mental processes.**

### 1. Scientific Study
Psychology is **not just guessing about people's feelings**. It uses:
- Experiments
- Observations
- Data & research methods

Just like Biology or Physics, psychologists test hypotheses and collect evidence.

**Examples:**
- Studying how sleep affects memory
- Studying how stress affects decision making

---

### 2. Behavior
**Behavior** means any **observable action** — anything that can be seen or measured.

Examples: talking, walking, studying, crying, smiling, typing code 💻

---

### 3. Mental Processes
Things happening **inside the mind** that can't be seen directly, but are studied through experiments and tests.

Examples: thinking, remembering, dreaming, feeling emotions, making decisions

---

> ✅ **In simple words: Psychology = Study of what people do and how they think.**

## 🏛️ History of Psychology

Psychology developed slowly over thousands of years, through **3 main stages**.

---

### Stage 1 — Philosophy Era (Before 1879)

Psychology was originally part of **Philosophy**. Thinkers tried to understand the mind, soul, and human thinking.

| Philosopher | Key Idea |
|-------------|----------|
| **Socrates** | *"Know thyself"* — examine your thoughts and beliefs |
| **Plato** | Knowledge is innate (born with us); mind and body are separate |
| **Aristotle** | Wrote *De Anima* (On the Soul); studied memory, learning, emotions, perception. Often called the **first real psychologist** |

---

### Stage 2 — Birth of Scientific Psychology (1879)

In **1879**, German scientist **Wilhelm Wundt** opened the **first psychology laboratory** at Leipzig University.

🏆 This is why he is called the **"Father of Modern Psychology."**

For the first time, the human mind was studied scientifically — officially separating psychology from philosophy.

---

### Stage 3 — Modern Psychology (1900 → Present)

After Wundt, different schools of thought emerged rapidly.

| Psychologist | Contribution |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Sigmund Freud** | Founded **Psychoanalysis** — unconscious mind controls behavior; childhood shapes personality |
| **John B. Watson** | Founded **Behaviorism** — psychology should only study observable behavior |
| **B.F. Skinner** | Expanded behaviorism; studied reinforcement and punishment |
| **Jean Piaget** | Studied how children develop thinking abilities |

---

## 🎯 The 4 Goals of Psychology

Every psychologist works toward these four goals:

1. **Describe** — What is happening?
2. **Explain** — Why is it happening?
3. **Predict** — What will happen next?
4. **Control/Influence** — How can we change it?
