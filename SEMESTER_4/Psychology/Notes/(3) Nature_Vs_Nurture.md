# 🧠 Nature vs. Nurture Debate

One of the most famous debates in psychology — appears in almost every introductory course.

---

## The Core Question

> **Are human behaviors caused by genetics or by the environment?**

| | Meaning |
|--|---------|
| 🧬 **Nature** | What we are **born with** (genetics) |
| 🌱 **Nurture** | What we **learn** from our environment |

---

## 1️⃣ Nature (Genetics)

Traits inherited from biological parents through **genes** — present from birth, rooted in DNA.

**Examples of things largely influenced by genetics:**
- Eye color & height
- Natural intelligence potential
- Certain personality traits
- Some mental disorders

> **Example:** If both parents are tall, the child is likely to be tall. Some researchers also believe intelligence has a genetic component.

---

## 2️⃣ Nurture (Environment)

The experiences and surroundings that **shape a person** over time.

Includes: family, education, culture, friends, social experiences, life events.

**Examples of things shaped by environment:**
- Language you speak
- Religious beliefs
- Study habits & manners
- Skills (coding, sports, music)

> **Example:** You were not born knowing Python or C++. You learned it through education and practice. That is nurture.

---

## 👤 Famous View — John B. Watson

Watson, founder of **Behaviorism**, made a bold claim:

> *"Give me a dozen healthy infants and I can train any one of them to become any type of specialist."*

He believed environment could shape almost **everything**.

---

## ⚖️ Nature vs. Nurture — Side by Side

| Nature | Nurture |
|--------|---------|
| Genes | Environment |
| Biological traits | Life experiences |
| Inherited | Learned |
| Present at birth | Developed over time |

---

## 🧠 Modern Psychology View

Today, psychologists agree:

> **Human behavior is shaped by both nature and nurture together.**

Genes provide **potential** — environment determines how that potential **develops**.

> **Example:** A child may have genetic potential for high intelligence, but without education and support, that potential may never fully develop.

---

## 💻 CS Analogy

Since you're a CS student, think of it this way:

| Psychology | Computing |
|------------|-----------|
| **Nature** | Hardware capability |
| **Nurture** | Software & programs installed |

Both are needed for the system to perform well.
