# 🧠 Major Schools of Thought in Psychology

Different "approaches" or "perspectives" psychologists developed to explain behavior and the mind.

---

## Overview Timeline

| School | Founder(s) | Focus |
|--------|------------|-------|
| **Structuralism** | Wundt, Titchener | Structure of the mind |
| **Functionalism** | William James | Purpose of mental processes |
| **Behaviorism** | Watson, Skinner | Observable behavior only |
| **Psychoanalysis** | Sigmund Freud | Unconscious mind |
| **Gestalt** | Max Wertheimer | Whole perception |
| **Humanistic** | Maslow, Rogers | Human potential & free will |
| **Cognitive** | Multiple | Thinking & information processing |

---

## 1️⃣ Structuralism

**Founder:** Wilhelm Wundt | **Expanded by:** Edward Titchener

**Idea:** Analyze the **structure of the human mind** by breaking mental experiences into basic components — similar to how chemistry breaks substances into elements.

**Method — Introspection:**
People describe their own thoughts, feelings, and sensations.

> **Example:** Someone tastes an apple and reports sweetness, texture, and smell.

**Why it declined:** Introspection was too **subjective** to be scientifically reliable.

---

## 2️⃣ Functionalism

**Founder:** William James | **Influenced by:** Charles Darwin's Theory of Evolution

**Idea:** Instead of studying the *structure* of the mind, ask — **what is the purpose of mental processes?**

Mental processes exist because they help humans **adapt to the environment**.

> **Example:** Humans feel fear because it helps us **survive dangerous situations**.

---

## 3️⃣ Behaviorism

**Founder:** John B. Watson | **Expanded by:** B.F. Skinner

**Idea:** Psychology should study **only observable behavior** — the mind can't be measured scientifically, but behavior can.

**Key concept:** Behavior is shaped by rewards, punishments, and environment.

> **Example:** A child studies more because they receive praise or good grades.

Highly influential in **learning theory and education**.

---

## 4️⃣ Psychoanalysis

**Founder:** Sigmund Freud

**Idea:** Much of our behavior is controlled by the **unconscious mind**, which contains:
- Hidden desires & fears
- Repressed memories
- Childhood experiences

Psychological problems often stem from **unresolved unconscious conflicts**.

**Famous concepts introduced:**

| Concept | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Id** | Primitive instincts and desires |
| **Ego** | Rational, conscious self |
| **Superego** | Moral conscience |

---

## 5️⃣ Gestalt Psychology

**Founder:** Max Wertheimer

**Core idea:**

> *"The whole is greater than the sum of its parts."*

Humans perceive **complete patterns**, not just separate pieces.

> **Example:** When you see a face, you don't process separate eyes, nose, and mouth — you instantly see a **whole face**.

Influenced modern studies of **perception and visual processing**.

---

## 6️⃣ Humanistic Psychology

**Key figures:** Abraham Maslow, Carl Rogers

**Idea:** Focuses on personal growth, free will, self-development, and human potential.

**Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs:**

```
5. Self-Actualization   ← reaching full potential
4. Esteem               ← confidence, achievement
3. Love & Belonging     ← relationships, connection
2. Safety               ← security, stability
1. Physiological        ← food, water, sleep
```

Humans must fulfill lower needs before progressing to higher ones.

---

## 7️⃣ Cognitive Psychology

One of the **most modern and influential** approaches today.

**Focus:** How the mind **processes information** — similar to how a computer processes data.

Studies: thinking, memory, problem solving, language, decision making.

**Connected fields:**
- Artificial Intelligence
- Neuroscience

---

## 💻 CS Connection

> Modern **AI and machine learning** are heavily inspired by Cognitive Psychology — both try to understand how **thinking and decision-making** work.
